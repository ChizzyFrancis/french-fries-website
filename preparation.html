How French Fries Are Prepared

Perfect fries require the famous double-fry method:

  1. Cut potatoes into uniform strips (7–10 mm)
  2. Blanch at 130–150°C for 5–8 minutes (cooks inside, no colour)
  3. Cool completely (or freeze for commercial fries)
  4. Second fry at 175–190°C for 2–4 minutes (golden & crispy)

Russet or Maris Piper potatoes are best due to high starch content. The Maillard reaction during the second fry creates the golden colour and flavour.
